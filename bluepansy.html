Blue Pansy

Updated On: 02 FEB 2023
© Federick Ho | butterflycircle.com

The male Blue Pansy is the prettiest, where its hindwing is a bright blue with an orange-red subtornal ocellus. The female has a sombre brown on the upperside. A better way to distinguish their sexes is by the size of the spots on the hindwing. The undersides of both sexes are pale brown.

Characteristics

Scientific name: Junonia Orithya

Habitat: Open areas on grassy patches

Favourite foods: Plant

Predator: Birds

Fun fact: This butterfly has a wingspan of about 45-60 mm and belongs to the largest butterfly family
